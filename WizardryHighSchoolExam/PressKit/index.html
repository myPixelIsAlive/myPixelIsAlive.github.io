Description

In a world where humans with magical abilities are destined to become powerful and respected wizards, welcome to the wizardry examination reception room. Here, students with diverse backgrounds are waiting for their turn. Some are determined and ready to face the challenge, while others have simply walked through the wrong door. Regardless of the reasons for their presence, they will all undergo the same, some might say "deadly," examination. Let's leave the naysayers to their fantasies. Remember this: everything is prepared. The dungeons have been cleaned and carry the scent of pine from the moors. The golems are activated and ready to assess everything that moves.


Well, let's be honest, very few students are truly prepared for this examination. To succeed, students have no choice but to push themselves and break their limits, all in just a short quarter of an hour. But let's remain optimistic, the school would not abandon its clients, I mean, its students. The school will undoubtedly show a special kind of kindness to this new class. Who's talking about cheating? No, no, no! I won't allow it!


History

Hi, my name is Florent: the solo developer behind the studio My Pixel is alive.

Initially, I started developing a game titled "Wizardry" for iOS (nothing to do with the eponymous series from the 80s, which I was not familiar with). However, the scope of this game began to drift, and I found myself adding a level editor, then developing visual programming tools for enemy behavior, and so on. In short, I was going off the rails. I decided to "pause this project" (meaning: "abandon this project") and start over with a project that had a manageable scope, achievable in just a few months. I chose to go with a roguelite-style game like Vampire Survivor. Now it was time to release a game! I kept the name "Wizardry" in the title, along with the magic. For the rest, I tried to add my personal touch. In the end, the initial estimated development time was at least doubled. But Wizardry High School Exam is here. When I compare it to the highly competitive market, I often feel overwhelmed. However, when I play it, I smile and think to myself, "Did I actually create this game? Cool!".


Features


  • Students have access to 6 types of magic: fire, ice, lightning, necromancy, telekinesis, and technomancy.


  • Each student can learn up to 4 minor spells that automatically target enemies.


  • Each student can learn a major spell that is cast manually.


  • The dungeons are scattered with very powerful one-time-use spells:


  • Trances allow each student to push their limits.


  • Calamities are summons of devastating natural powers.


  • Speed rounds: The exam lasts less than 15 minutes per student.


  • The exam requires surviving twelve dungeons, randomly chosen from a pool of around twenty.


  • Collect money and gems to purchase artifacts that will "aid" in the exam. It's a bit like cheating, well, kind of...


  • Collect experience orbs to enhance your abilities and learn new spells.
